  * you get a bonus when using a cold spell

* Dragons
  * from weakest to strongest: Young < Young Adult < Adult < Old < Very Old < Ancient
  * Each variety has a different constitution: Green < While < Blue < Red
  * has three melee attacks (tail, claws and bite) and a breath attack
  * grows in ability to dodge with age (increased intelligence)
  * probability of breath attack increases with age
  * severity of breath attack increases with age
  * severity of melee attack increases with age
  * Red Dragons
    * fire attack
    * resistant to fire spells
    * susceptible to cold spells
  * Blue Dragons
    * lightening attack
    * unknown resistance and susceptability
  * White Dragons
    * cold attack
    * resistant to cold spells
    * susceptible to fire spells
  * Green Dragons
    * poison attack (effects of poison is gradual)
    * susceptible to lightening attacks
* Devils
  * resistant *but not immune* to cold magic
  * immune to fire magic
  * standard damage from lightening magic
  * ability to teleport in allies when attacked (probability based)
  * Spiked < Horned < Ice < Abyss 
  * Spiked Devils
    * do not use weapons to enhance their attack
    * tail and claw melee attack
  * Horned Devils
    * probability based fire attack
    * may have low grade weapon to enhance melee attack
    * enhanced dexterity
  * Ice Devils
    * probability based Ice attack
    * unlike other Devils, they are susceptible to fire magic (you get a bonus, they are not immune)
    * may have low grade weapon to enhance melee attack
  * Abyss Fiends
    * probability of fire attack
    * ability to summon *any* class of devil (probability based), not just the same class
    * may have *any* grade weapon to enhance melee attack
    * may have armor to enhance defense
    * cannot defeat these without destroying armor unlike other monsters that have armor. 
* Elementals
  * Air Elementals
    * May cause short range transportation effect with damage in place of attack
    * melee attack takes the form of slamming opponent into walls/columns (immovable dungeon features)
  * Earth Elementals
    * Known for breaking doors
    * (low) Probability of passing through solid walls. 
  * Fire Elementals
    * No melee attack
    * fire attack instead 
    * fire attack is *not* a ranged effect unlike other fire magic. 
* Water Elementals
  * Similar to Air Elementals
  * Additional ability to drown opponent (low probability)
* Humanoids with in Game Help
  * Kobold
    * power grows with count attacking
    * very low power when attacking alone
  * Goblins
    * roughly equivalent to a balanced L1 player. 
    * May have a low level weapon
    * May have a small amount of copper coins
  * Goblin Fighers
    * roughly equivalent to a balanced L3 player. 
    * Will have a weapon.
      * probability of a mid level weapon
      * low probability of an enchanted weapon
    * May have low level armor
    * low probability of a small to medium amount of copper coins
    * very low probability of a small amount of silver coins (independent of copper coins probability)
    * low probability of carrying a pack (may be enchanted). Low probability this pack is undamaged. 
  * Hobgoblins - Goblin Fighter with
    * much higher probability of armor
      * 50% probability armor is damaged
    * 10x probability of having coins
    * less constitution
    * less attack
    * slightly higher dexterity
  * Ogres
    * Usually has armor
      * armor is usually damaged
    * frequently has a helmet
      * helmet may be damaged
    * Has a weapon
      * carries at least a club
      * frequently a spear 
      * weapon will occasionally be a mid grade weapon
      * weapon may be enchanted (low probability)
      * very low probability of a high grade weapon
    * frequently carries gold and/or silver coins (10-50 pieces on average)
    * slightly susceptible to cold magic
    * roughly equivalent to a balanced L5 player 
  * Thieves
    * weak attack
    * ability to attack more than once per turn
    * probability of removing coins from your purse *per attack* 
    * probability of removing your purse per turn
    * probability of removing your pack *if stored on belt* per turn
    * cannot take a belt worn in the belt slot, but if the belt has slots, can remove things from the slots. 
    * if something is removed from the character's belt slot, the message "Something seems missing" is displayed.
    * if something is removed from the character's purse slot, either coins or the entire purse, the messsage "your purse feels lighter" is displayed.
    * Will randomly vanish instead of continuing an attack. Same effects (including distance) as a teleport
    * Able to pass through hidden doors unlike other monsters
    * high probability of changing dungeon level if near stairs
    * slightly susceptible to fire magic
    * always drops a purse when defeated.  The purse never contains less than the amount stollen from you during the attack. 
    * always drops any other items stollen during an attack. 
    * items stollen are tracked *per thief* -- there may be many thieves spawned across the various map levels at any given point. 
  * Trolls
    * susceptible to fire attacks
    * slight regenerative ability 
    * up to 2 melee attacks per turn
    * roughly equivalent to a balanced player at level 7 with armor rating at about 75
  * Wizards
    * low constitution
    * high mana (compared to a player)
    * high dexterity
    * able to cast any spell in the game
    	* including the summon monster spell
    	* including the slow player spell
    	* only the 'necromancer' variety can cast 'Summon Undead'
    * can drop *any* non-unique item. 
    * frequently drop enchanted items. 
    * often drop valuable & rare potions and scrolls
  	* usually drop multiple items. 
  * Undead
    * Ghost
      * probability of a drain strength attack
      * probability of a drain dexterity attack
      * drain attacks are *not* ranged
      * drain attacks are temporary
      * drain attacks can be fatal
      * frequently pass through solid walls/doors
      * slight 'homing' capability once in line of sight of a player (movement will probably be roughly towards the player but may not take the most optimal path).
      * almost never changes levels. 
      * drops a single random item
      * can attack a ghost in a wall via magic
      * Items dropped in a wall (if the ghost is defeated while in a wall, ghost still drops a single item) are permenently lost
    * Shadow < Shade < Spectre
    	* all use the same graphic, but different alt text

    	* use the weapon for melee attacks
    	* have same drain attack as ghost
    	  * probability increases with class.
    	  * Spectres have same probability as ghost
    	* can *not* pass through walls (carries a weapon)
    	  * can *retreat* into a wall
    	  * loses the melee attack till it shares a space with a weapon (and picks it up)

    	* slightly susceptible to melee attacks

  	* Skeletons < Walking Corpses

# Bosses

Bosses are non-standard unique varients of monsters that mark both milestones in the game play as well as in the game's narrative elements. 

* Hrugnir
 	* non-standard Hill Giant 
 	* alt text: Hill Giant Lord
 	* 11th level of the Fortress
 	* always appears with a group of other Orges
 	* ~ 6 Cold Balls or ~ 5 Ball Lightenings to defeat
 	* his ranged attack has a reasonable probability of missing independent of your dexterity
 	* his ranged attack is *slightly* stronger than a standard Hill Giant
* Utgardhalok
  * non-standard Hill Giant found in the Castle
  * alt text: "Hill Giant King"
  * always appears with a group of Hill Giants
  * his ranged attack has a slight probability of missing independent of your dexterity
  * his ranged attack is *slightly* stronger than Hrugnir's
* Rungir
  * non-standard Stone Giant
  * alt text: "Stone Giant King"
  * always appears with a group of Stone Giants
  * slightly stronger than average Stone Giant
* Thyrm
	* non-standard Ice Giant
  * alt text: "Ice Giant King"
  * always appears with a group of Ice Giants
  * slightly stronger than average Ice Giant
* Thiassa
  * non-standard Fire Giant
  * alt text: "Fire Giant King"
  * always appears with a group of Fire Giants
  * slightly stronger than average Fire Giant
* Surtur
  * non-standard Abyss Fiend
  * alt text: "Demon Lord"
  * appears with a random mix of all Devil types
  * slightly stronger than the average Abyss Fiend
  * slightly higher probability of summoning devils than the average Abyss Fiend
* Prisoner
  * this isn't a monster to defeat, but someone to rescue
  * shows up as, and reacts as a monster with no attack ability
    * detect monster will detect the prisoner
    * melee attacks will kill it
    * magic will harm it
    * highly susceptible to fire including proximity effects
    * ~ 2 Cold Ball to kill 
  * appears at level 4 of the Castle
  * once you hit level 4, you see 'You hear somebody screaming for help!'
  * once every 10 minutes after you will get additional messages (ordered list)
    1. You hear screams echoing through the halls!
    1. You hear drums beating ominously!
    1. The drums are beating faster!
    1. You hear guttural voices chanting!
    1. You hear a voice scream 'Please! Somebody help me!'
    1. You hear a voice scream 'No! Stop! The knife! Ahhhhh!'
  * If the last message is shown, the event stops, 
    * the prisoner disappears
    * you continue the game but without the reward
    * the Ogres start to move around the Castle randomly (both up and down levels)
  * when you reach the room with the prisoner (there are several Orges in the room)
    * the message 'Free me, please, noble one!' is displayed
    * the Ogres move to attack you
  * If you get to the prisoner before the Ogres either kill you or him (they will do so if a least distance path to you passes within one square of the prisoner)
    * There is a story window with some (unknown to me at the current time) info
    * On your next visit to the town, you can visit the Jarl who will give you 100 pieces of gold
    * The town merchants will offer *slightly* better prices 
  * Falling below level 4 via a trap door will trigger the start of the event, but you will not see any of the messages unless on level 4.
  * this is one of the only things in the game affected by wall clock. 
  * saving and quitting pauses the 10 min timer.  Leaving the game open but not taking turns does *not* pause the clock. 
  * Prisoner is freed when all enemies in Line of Sight are defeated. 
  * It is easy to accidentally kill the prisoner by accidentally melee attacking him, or via proximity effects of multi-square spells
