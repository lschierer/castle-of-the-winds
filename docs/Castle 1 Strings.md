#You gaze once more at the charred ruins of the farm where you were raised.
You buried the blackened skeletons of your godparents in the remains of
the garden they loved; but you can't bury the anger which still seethes at
the thought of how they died.  Grimly, you vow that nothing will prevent
you from avenging their deaths.
The marauders pillaged the farm quite thoroughly.  Nowhere in the ruins
can you find the amulet left for you by your true father, whose
dying words, whispered to your godfather, were supposedly of its
importance to you: of how it could lead you to your fortune and great
glory, but only if you proved your worth.  Your godparents had promised it
to you for your 18th birthday; now you have neither godparents nor
birthright, and your birthday has just passed.
A search for clues in the rubble finds only a confused trail of footprints,
leading north, towards the mountains.  Many of the footprints seem much
too large to have come from the boots of bandits or soldiers.
You look north, wondering:  Where might the amulet be by now?  To whom
must you prove yourself, and how?
#You examine the scrap of paper carefully, which turns
out to be part of a message in a strange blood red script.
The top part is missing, but you can make out the following:
          ...is dead, return to the fortress north of Bjarnarhaven and
          await my orders.  I repeat, stop at NOTHING to ensure this
          danger is removed!
It is signed at the bottom with a single ornate 'S', with
flames entwining the letter.  As you stare at the flames
they seem to flicker and dance, and you feel the paper grow
hot in your hands.  You hurriedly drop it as the paper bursts
into flames, and you watch in shock as the ashes fall to the
ground.  Your stomach feels a bit queasy with worry,
and you think maybe you should head back to the hamlet.
#You are almost home.  Ahead of you, the path winds another half
mile around the hills north of the hamlet.  Your pack rests heavily
on your shoulders, shifting slightly with each step you take.  The
enigmas of the mine still nag at you: why were those fell creatures
encamped therein?  From where did they come?  And what was the meaning
of the scrap of parchment you recovered at the bottom?
As you round the last hill, a sharp smoky smell fills your nostrils.
Burning thatch?  Hastily, you drop your pack and jog to the wrecked
gate.  Heavy smoke hangs in the air; charred timbers still smolder from
the ruins of houses.  The air lies eerily quiet, missing the babble
of a living hamlet: the cries of children, the cackle of poultry.  A
wrecked wagon lies, overturned, in the middle of the road.  Two vultures
start at your intrusion, and flap away heavily.
With horror, you suddenly understand part of the message fragment you
found in the mine:
          ...is dead, return to the fortress north of Bjarnarhaven and
          wait my orders.  I repeat: stop at nothing to ensure that
          this danger is removed!
#This was no random act of destruction, and neither was the burning of
your farm.  Somebody ordered this, somebody who saw danger in this
humble hamlet... or in its inhabitants.
Shocked, you realize that this savage act must have been aimed at you.
None of these villagers had traveled more than a league from home in
their entire lives.  You were not born here, but far away; you lost
your godparents in a similar horrific act of arson.  You must be the
danger!  You wonder again at your unknown past, which again has proven
deadly to those you loved; and you swear once again to exact vengeance
against those responsible.
Once more you ponder the scrap of parchment.  The town of Bjarnarhaven lies
but a day's journey down the highway to the west; perhaps you should
exercise your growing skills against this "fortress."  You kick aside a
piece of broken gate, then, remembering your dropped pack, head back up
the path to recover it.  Bjarnarhaven awaits you.
#You uncrumple and carefully examine the scrap of parchment,
finding it covered with crude handwriting in a dark
reddish-brown ink.  Laboriously, you decipher the writing:
          Take a Squad of Goblin Warriorf & patrol ye upper
          Levelf of ye Fortrefs carefully.  There be Signf of an
          Intruder within ye Dunjon; & HRUNGNIR fearf we may have
          mifsed our Quarry.  As HRUNGNIR haf already carried hif
          Tale of Succefs back to ye Caftle, we muft labor to
          bring the Ring of Truth to hif Word; elfe the leaping
          Flames will claim uf.
          Should ye find human Men or Women lyrking about, slay
          them without Delay; & bring uf the stilled Bodief.
          Loot not their Corpfes! or elfe tafte the leaping Flames.
The terse note prompts more questions than it answers:  Who
is this Hrungnir?  And what castle is this mentioned, to
which Hrungnir reports?  Somebody ordered a dragnet;
uneasily, you suspect that you were the target, just as you
were the target of your village's arson.  Who is
masterminding this search?
Grimly, you laugh as you shred the note: at least this
patrol will now never loot against orders.
#As you examine the contents of the bandit's chests, you
realize with a shock that some of what is here came from your
farm and village!  You recognize cloths and merchandise
from the general store, and even a battered shield that hung
over the blacksmith's forge!  You root through the chests,
hoping against hope that your father's amulet is there, but
are disappointed.
You know for sure, however, that you are on the right track.
These marauders you defeated must surely have been responsible
for torching your home, and you feel a savage joy at repaying
them in kind.  You aren't done yet, you know, for there is still
this Hrungnir to deal with, but his time of reckoning is fast
approaching!
#Before you looms a gigantic figure out of legend.  Long ago
in a more peaceful time, your godparents spun tales of the
Nine Worlds around you: the worlds of the Aesir and Vanir
gods above; the world of the dead and the dark dwarfs below;
and grim worlds of frost and flame and stone through which
stride the mighty Jotuns, called Giants in those rare times
when they walk the Earth.  The enormous being before you can
only be such a Jotun: taller and broader than any man, or
even any ogre, yet equipped and accoutered in a manner which
suggests intelligence much beyond any mere monster.
Eyes which gleam with evil meet your gaze.  The giant rubs
his broad chin and shouts in a gravelly bass:
         Who is the impertinent human who would visit great
         Hrungnir with a weapon drawn?
At last!  You confront the one who destroyed your foster
parents' farm, who burned and pillaged the simple hamlet
which sheltered you, who in his pursuit of you has
slaughtered all you held dear.

#Hrungnir notes your mounting rage and chuckles:
         Ah.  So you are the one I was to kill, the little
         creature who caused such a disturbance in the upper
         fortress... I suspected that those villagers were too
         meek to have been the threat I sought.  How kind of you
         now to come to me so that I may finish the task properly.
         My lord need never know of my initial failure.
         Guards!  Destroy this foolish pest!
An imperious gesture from Hrungnir summons a squad of armored ogres.
As they rush at you, the giant hefts a round boulder in one hand.
#Hrungnir grimaces in annoyance, and with a quick gesture
wards your spell, which shatters into coruscating sparks.
He smirks and growls,
          So the little hero is a mage as well.  How clever.
          Spare yourself the effort of your tiny human spells; I
          had tasted enchantments which would boil your blood
          before your father's father ever knew life.  And my

You redouble your efforts in the face of the giant's pompous
laughter.
#The mortally wounded Hrungnir staggers and falls to one
knee, descending to your eye level.  One huge hand points at
you; you brace for a counterspell as the giant traces a
serpentine pattern with a carmine glowing finger, hissing
sibilantly.  Suddenly Hrungnir coughs and chokes, and the
spell flickers out prematurely.  He gasps and meets your
gaze again:
          My lord... thinks that you... dead.
A short bark of giantish laughter dies into a rattle as
Hrungnir's eyes glaze, and he pitches over, dead.
Vengeance is yours.
With patience and courage worthy of the great Aesir, you
have wrought revenge for yourself, your foster parents, and
your village.  All the pain of the recent weeks came from
the designs of the enormous being Hrungnir; now his body
lies dead before you.  His depredations have come to an end.
But who sent this Hrungnir?  To whom or what did he report?
Why were you selected to receive his malign attentions?
#These questions haunt you as, impelled by a nagging
suspicion, you search the battered body of the dead giant.
A familiar glint from under the giant's jerkin stops your
breath momentarily.  There!  With trembling hands you draw
forth a shining amulet, which warms immediately to your
touch.  Left for you by your dead and unknown father, kept
in trust for you by your slain foster parents, stolen by
Hrungnir's brigands, now recovered by your heroism: you now
hold in your hands the promised legacy, the key to your
unknown birthright.  It seems to glow faintly in greeting,
and grows unnaturally warm as you think of your father.
Without hesitation you place the amulet around your neck,
where it fits you perfectly.
#Upon activating the amulet, you immediately feel it radiate power.  As its
aura spreads, enveloping you, your awareness of the world around you fades.
A warm, colorless glow obscures your vision; a watery rushing sound fills
your ears; you can no longer feel the weight of your armor, or even your
feet on the ground, as if you were floating in a warm pool.  The rushing
sound fades to a low burble, and then to a barely noticeable throb.  You feel
warm, and protected, and somehow content.
Time passes.  A minute?  An hour?  You don't know.  After an unknown time,
you notice a shape in the glow before you; as you watch, it coalesces into
the form of a man in shimmering ornate armor, who meets your gaze and smiles
warmly.  His features seem familiar, though you don't recognize his face.
He opens his mouth to speak, and the low throb in your ears rises rapidly in
pitch until it forms words:
         My %x, I am he who was called Prince Arvi, thy father.  The power of the
         amulet which thou now dost wear has summoned me from the nether realm,
         and thee from Midgard, so that I may speak to thee this once.  Thou hast
         done very well to have come this far against all the obstacles before thee,
         and I am well pleased with the manner in which thou hast shown thy mettle,
         avenging the deaths of thine godparents and the destruction of thy village.
         Yet thy greatest test still awaits thee.
#Elation fills you at your father's words of praise, and the discovery of
your royal blood; but his hint of future trials tempers your delight with
foreboding.  Your father continues:
         Eighteen winters ago, disaster struck our line, when we became enmeshed in

         It is thy task, then, as the last surviving heir to the throne, and thy fated
         destiny, as my %x, to thwart Surtur's designs on Midgard.  Thou must
         reclaim our fallen Castle of the Winds!
#         When thou dost invoke the power of thy amulet once more, it will transport
         thee to a town near the Castle.  From there, wend thy way to the Castle's

You notice your father's voice slur somewhat, dropping a bit in pitch, and
his mailed legs waver as if seen through a fire.  He looks away for a
moment, then meets your gaze again.
         I may tarry here no longer, but must leave thee now to fulfill thy destiny and
         so lift the doom on our bloodline.  Thy grandfather can tell thee more.  May all
         the Aesir shield thee, dear %x.
With a final gentle smile, your father's image dissolves into the
surrounding glow, and a low rumble replaces his voice in your ears,
leaving you alone, once again fatherless.  The glow reveals no other
figures, yields no other voices.  You float without sense of time.
After an indeterminable period, awareness of your surroundings returns.
You find yourself back on your feet, with the Amulet cooling in your hand,
and a hundred questions heating your mind.
